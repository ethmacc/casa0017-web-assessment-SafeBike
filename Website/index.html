About SafeBike

In 2023/2024 there were 66,960 reported incidents of bicycle theft across England and Wales. Even though this represents a slight drop from the 2022/2023 figures of 77,170, it remains a signifcant issue. If left unaddressed, this crime could potentially discourage people from cycling in London, undermining efforts to promote sustainable transportation and reduced carbon emissions in the capital. Through SafeBike, we aim to inform people of bicycle theft hotspots, so cyclists can take better preventative measures against the crime. SafeBike is a web application that innovatively visualises bicycle theft crime statistics in London through a visually interactive map. This is powered by a data driven backend that utilises Node.js, allowing users to access historic Metropolitan Police Data from the last 24 months specifically on bicycle theft. These statistics are broken down into Lower Layer Super Output Areas (LSOAs) and Boroughs, making it easier to identify different trends and patterns of bike theft in London.

Key Features

Find The Safest Parking Spots

SafeBike will inform users when they enter an area with high levels of bicycle theft, helping them make better informed decisions on the safest place to park their bikes. By identifying these hotspots cyclists can reduce their likelihood of becoming victims of bicycle theft.

Accurate and Reliable Routing

SafeBike has a routing function to allow you to navigate to safer parking spots within the borough you are in. Simply type in the address or postcode of your desired location and SafeBike will swiftly and accurately guide you to your destination!

Precisely Filter Data

You have the ability to filter bicycle theft data by Borough. Therefore, you can view speicifc statistics within the borough you are located in. In addition, you have the option to filter historical data up to the last 24 months.
